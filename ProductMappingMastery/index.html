How this connects to design controls: The Control vs. Noise distinction comes from robust design methods (P-diagrams, Taguchi). It matters for design controls because Control factors become design input requirements and specifications — things your team specifies, documents, and verifies. Noise factors define the use conditions and ranges your requirements must cover (patient variation, user variation, environment) and drive your verification and validation strategy.

A note on Game 2's name: the "output statements" you assemble are functional requirement statements — the functional result each use step must produce. These feed your design inputs (21 CFR 820.30(c)). Don't confuse them with formal design outputs (820.30(d)), which are the drawings, specifications, and procedures that result from design work.
